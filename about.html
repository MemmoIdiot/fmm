Francesco Maria Messina

Francesco Maria Messina was born in Pisa, Italy where he undertook classical studies. After attending the École Spéciale d’Architecture in Paris, he received his master's degree, graduating in 2015 from the innovative Confluence InsEtute in Lyon, France, founded by Odile Decq and Matteo Cainer. He then went on to have a brief experience at Tulane University in New Orleans, USA, before continuing his international experience abroad, initally working in Paris and then in Cameroon, Africa. Between 2018 and 2020, Francesco became a fully fledged designer conceiving and creating five design lines, while also partecipating in the launch of a new luxury furniture brand named Cypraea, a Mauritian brand of which he was the creator and brand manager. His works were an immediate success and were exhibited at Rossana Orlandi’s Gallery in Milan and Porto Cervo. In the same year, he also exhibited in London. In 2020, strengthened by this very successful experience, he returned to Tuscany and opened his own design studio. FMM focuses on the pressing global issues through a creative and material dialogue between nature and design, and between design and materiality. His new works were displayed at Rossana Orlandi’s Gallery in Milan and Porto Cervo and in 2021 at the Venice Design Biennale and the Arte in Nuvola in Rome. In France, he is represented by Galerie des Lyons in Paris. A new partnership was signed in Oceania with Bonham gallery. Francesco's works have been published in various books and design magazines. They have also been exhibited internationally and are currently part of various private collections.

Authentic craftmanship

All of our pieces are meticulously crafted by expert artisans in Tuscany, Italy, in locations such as Carrara, Pisa, Volterra, and Florence, where there is great artistic and historical value, as well as a fine tradition of craftsmanship dating back to Etruscan times. We produce only a maximum of nine items in each series to maintain their exclusivity and preciousness, with most of the work being handmade to add uniqueness. Our goal is to offer our collectors items they can truly be proud of - authentic, rare, and one-of-a-kind designs. I believe in the value of authentic craftmanship and unique materials and the driving force of my design practice is always the development of challenging concepts and the pursuite of original, made-to-art cutting edge designs which challenge trends and commercial movements.

fmm mission

“When designing, great importance is given to contemporary global issues like global warming and ocean plastic pollution, to which the collections Glacies (dedicated to polar and Alpine ice melting) and Re-plustic (made of 100% recycled plastic) are directly referred to and aim to raise awareness about these tragic issues. Just like climate change, coastal erosion, and polar ice melting, through the other collections I have developed in the last 5 years, my personal mission is to raise awareness about one of the most pressing global issues of our time: plastic pollution. My personal commitment to this cause began many years ago Between 2017 and 2020, during my time in the Indian Ocean, particularly in Mauritius, I witnessed firsthand the devastating impact of plastic pollution on the marine ecosystem and on the shores. I regularly participated in clean-up actions and tried to promote campaigns with locals to stimulate and raise awareness about this issue. As a personal experience in Africa, during my years spent in Cameroon, I also encountered many communities that were unaware of the gravity of their actions and uneducated about the global issue of plastic pollution. They used to regularly dump their trash on the roads or in the rivers. In that case, it wasn’t a lack of goodwill but more of unawareness. That is why I am committed to using my work to raise awareness and contribute educating people about this critical issue. To address this problem, I began developing design objects made from recycled plastic waste to transform something ugly and harmful into something beautiful and functional. One of my recent pieces is the "refloatable" console, made of buoys 3D-printed from 100% recycled plastic. I believe that creating aesthetically appealing and fun designs can help spread the message about the urgent need to combat plastic pollution, even in the field of collectible design, which is still relatively new to adopting sustainable practices. Until recent years, recycled plastic design was often seen as a lower-quality form of design, but that perception is changing as more people recognize the importance of sustainability in all areas of life.”
